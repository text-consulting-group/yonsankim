Yongsan Garrison was not sustained by fences and gates alone. Who passed through it, what ceremonies were staged there, and which buildings became central all contributed to the base's authority. Photographs produced by the Eighth U.S. Army and other military units preserve those operating scenes.

Images of gates show boundaries. Headquarters buildings and official ceremonies reveal forms of command and display. These photographs do more than tell us what once stood in Yongsan. They also reveal who believed a given scene was worth recording, and under what institutional gaze that judgment was made.

To read Yongsan's record photographs, then, is to read both landscape and the system that produced the record. This is why `sources` and `creators` matter so much. Once we follow the producing organization, we begin to see that even the same place could be photographed from very different positions and for very different purposes.
