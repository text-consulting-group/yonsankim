The Yongsan archive is not meant to be read as a single genre. Military record photographs, aerial images, field documentation, seminars, and public discussions all live inside the same corpus. Because of that, it helps to begin with a small bundle of references rather than with a single keyword search.

If the goal is to see original materials first, `1. Original Records` and the `sources` taxonomy are the best place to start. Producing organizations reveal the administrative order of the record. From there, `2. Base and City Walks` makes it possible to read place and urban change through curated series. Finally, `3. Kim's Archive` opens the present-day public discourse through seminars, debates, and field events.

This is not yet a finished bibliography. It is closer to a guide for sequencing attention inside the archive. Even so, that sequence already changes how the corpus appears. Yongsan stops being a single topic and becomes a layered field where different kinds of records overlap and speak to one another.
