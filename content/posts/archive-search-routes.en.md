The current Yongsan archive cannot be read through a single route. `1. Original Records` follows the administrative order of military records, `2. Base and City Walks` follows a curated urban-reading sequence, and `3. Kim's Archive` holds records of today's public discussions. Because the corpus is layered, search paths also need to change depending on the question.

When looking for original military photographs, record groups, producing organizations, and creators are usually the first stable anchors. By contrast, when reading city walks and field documentation, series titles and venue keywords become much more important. For seminars and discussion records, dates and event names are often the most precise entry points.

Given the current quality of the metadata, the most reliable sequence is still simple: top folder, then series or record group, then producing organization, then free search. Once this order becomes familiar, the 473 items stop feeling like a single undifferentiated mass and begin to separate into meaningful research clusters.
