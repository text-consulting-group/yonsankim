This page explains how to search and navigate the current corpus without requiring the user to read the exhibition first. The exhibition offers a reading sequence; this guide offers a finding sequence.

## Start with the top folders

### 1. Original Record Files

Use this zone when you want military record photographs and institutionally produced images. The strongest entry points are:

- record groups
- source organizations
- producers
- dates

### 2. Guided Walks through the U.S. Base and the City

Use this zone when you want curated routes, place-based sequences, and exhibition-linked materials. Here, `series` and `venues` are often stronger than record-group logic.

### 3. Kim's Historical Lens

Use this zone when you want seminars, public discussion, site visits, and present-day interpretation.

## Use metadata axes together

The most useful axes right now are:

- `series`
- `sources`
- `venues`
- `creators`
- `date`

`venues` is now filled across the live item set, but broad terms such as `Yongsan` are less useful than more specific venues like `Yongsan Garrison`, `Ichon-dong`, `Camp Kim`, or `Dragon Hill Lodge`.

## If you want to read through the exhibition

Go to:

- `/exhibition/`
- then choose `section-01` through `section-07`

Each section now expands into place-based item groupings, so the exhibition is also a route into the archive.

## Recommended order of discovery

1. Pick the top folder first.
2. Narrow by `series` or record group.
3. Use `sources`, `venues`, `creators`, `date`, or `media type`.
4. Finish with keyword search across title, summary, and description.
